## 🎤 SCRIPT

---

### [0:00 – 0:20] INTRO — Face on camera, confident

> "Hi, I'm [Your Name], currently in my [year] year at [College Name], CGPA [X.X].
>
> My favorite accomplishment? I built and shipped [one honest thing you're proud of — a project, app, or moment].
>
> Today I built AEO Diagnostic — a tool that answers one question every Amazon seller should be asking right now: when a shopper asks an AI assistant 'what's the best [your product]' — does YOUR brand even show up?"

---

### [0:20 – 1:30] DEMO — Screen share ON, talk through it naturally

> "Let me show you. I'm on the live app."

**[Type query: "best magnesium supplement for seniors"]**
**[Type brand: "Nature Made"]**
**[Click Run AEO Diagnostic]**

> "Watch this — it's querying three AI models simultaneously right now. Claude Haiku, Gemini 1.5 Flash, and Llama 3.3. Real API calls. Not mocked."

**[Loading screen animates through the steps]**

> "Okay — results are in."

**[Results page loads]**

> "So Nature Made gets a B — 68 out of 100. That means it shows up in 2 out of 3 AI models when shoppers ask this question. Rank #3 on average."

**[Scroll to Per-Model Breakdown]**

> "Here's the breakdown. Gemini ranks it at #2. Llama ranks it at #4. Gemma didn't mention it at all — that's a gap.
>
> Scroll down — here are the competitors beating it across all three models. [Competitor name] appears in all 3. That's the target."

**[Scroll to Recommendations]**

> "And here are 4 specific, actionable things to fix it — generated by Llama — with impact and effort ratings. Build review volume, get listed on Wirecutter, optimize the listing title. This is the actual work."

---

### [1:30 – 1:55] WHY I DESIGNED IT THIS WAY — Camera back on

> "I asked myself: who is the user?
>
> It's an Amazon seller. They don't care about API calls or token counts. They care about one thing: am I winning or losing?
>
> So I led with a single letter grade and one number. Like an SEO score — but for AI search. Then I gave them a ranked list of who's beating them, because sellers think in terms of competition. And I ended with actions, not just data.
>
> The whole flow is: score → why → fix it."

---

### [1:55 – 2:30] HOW IT WORKS + APIs — Technical but tight

> "Technically — the backend is Node.js Express. When you hit analyze, it fires three parallel API calls using Promise.all. No sequential waiting.
>
> **API 1 — Anthropic, Claude Haiku.** Free tier. Handles two jobs: answering the shopper query, and generating the 4 recommendations at the end.
>
> **API 2 — Google Gemini 1.5 Flash.** Free tier — 1 million tokens a day. Answers the same query independently.
>
> **API 3 — Groq again, but Claude Haiku 9B.** Google's model running on Groq's inference. Also free.
>
> Each response gets parsed — I extract brand mentions, their position in the ranked list, and frequency. Position at rank 1 gives 100 points, rank 2 gives 85, and so on. Mention bonus adds up to 20 more. Average the three models — that's your AEO score.
>
> Total cost to run one analysis: zero dollars."

---

### [2:30 – 2:55] IF I HAD MORE TIME

> "If I had more time — four things.
>
> One: weekly tracking. Run the same query every week, store scores in Supabase, show a trend line. Is your AEO improving?
>
> Two: add Perplexity and Claude to the model roster. They're the ones shoppers actually use.
>
> Three: connect to the Amazon listing directly. Paste your ASIN, it reads your listing, and auto-suggests keyword changes to improve AI visibility.
>
> Four: email alerts. If your brand drops out of the top 5 in any model, you get notified."

> "AEO is the next SEO. Pixii.ai's sellers need this. Thanks for watching."
